## 📈 PATTERNS & RECOMMENDATIONS

### What the most reliable candidates look like (based on market analysis):
- **They respond to ads rather than cold-messaging** — initiative = reliability signal
- **They mention specific experience** ("I cleaned offices for 2 years at XYZ") vs. vague ("I'm a hard worker")
- **They confirm availability immediately** — no hedging about the schedule
- **They ask practical questions** ("Where are the job sites?" "What supplies do you provide?") — shows they're thinking about actually doing the work
- **Bilingual candidates** (English/Spanish) tend to have strong community ties and are less likely to ghost — they were often referred by someone

### Recommendations to improve hiring success:
1. **Post bilingual ads** — the OC cleaning workforce is heavily Spanish-speaking. The bilingual post above will significantly expand your candidate pool.
2. **Respond within 2 hours** — in this market, good candidates are gone in 24-48 hours. Speed wins.
3. **Do a paid trial shift** — invite top candidates for one paid evening. You'll know immediately if they're a fit. Cheaper than a bad hire.
4. **Offer a $1/hr raise at 90 days** — even a small scheduled raise dramatically improves retention. ($18 → $19, $20 → $21, etc.)
5. **Ask for referrals early** — "Know anyone else who'd be good?" Reliable people know reliable people.
6. **Post on Sundays** — Craigslist and Facebook posts get the most eyes Sunday evening and Monday morning.
